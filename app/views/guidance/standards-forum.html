Purpose of the forum

The goal of the standards forum is to maintain a single, accessible framework of digital, data and technology standards for DfE, so specialists in teams can design and deliver compliant services, efficiently.

What the standards forum does

Ensure effective oversight and reporting of all standards with clear accountability.

The forum does this through the following actions.

Approving standards

The forum ensures standards are seamlessly integrated across all aspects of digital, data and technology in DfE.

They assess whether standards are written clearly and can be understood.

Reviewing standards

The forum periodically review existing standards and exceptions. This ensures standards remain current, relevant, and achievable.

Standards should evolve as needed to adapt to changing circumstances.

Ensure access to standards

The forum will make sure all users have easy access to the established standards.

Transparency and availability are essential for compliance.

Who is in the forum

Core members include Heads of Profession. The chair of each forum will rotate between the core members.

General attendees depends on what standards are being reviewed. A meeting usually includes profession leads, representatives from the Service Health Check App (opens DfE intranet) and Deputy Directors (DDs) sponsoring relevant standards.

Standards forum meetings

The standards forum meets once a week.

The forum are alerted to standards being submitted for review via the email address ddt.standards@education.gov.uk.

The minumum number of people in the forum is 2 core members. This includes a Head of Profession and a profession lead relevant to the standards being reviewed.

Standards forum agenda:

  • review standards
  • review escalated exceptions
  • any other business (AOB)

What the forum will review

New standards and any supporting material. Standards must be submitted via the standards manual (interim arrangements may be emailed a Word document).

Published standards that require re-review, as determined by their review date on the standards manual.

What the forum will output

Approved standards, with agreements to publish on the standards manual.

Recommendations to standards owners for any amendments.

Recommendations on standard creation or priority areas.

Escalations to Digital, Data, Technology senior leadership team or design and delivery board.
